(2020 - 2022)

Thesis: Ransomware detection using machine learning.

 Ransomwares are a huge threat to our society, indeed computing has become an important part of our daily lives, lots of information is stored on it, such as family photos, important documents, personal projects and more sensitive informations in the case of enterprises and state establishments. Additionally, cryptocurrencies have become more popular, making ransom collection easier and almost risk-free. Ransomware has evolved in order to implement techniques to avoid detection, among these techniques, the pre-attack phase, which aims to analyze the execution environment before launching the attack, in our study we used the Ransomware behavior during this phase through the API calls. We collected 631 ransomware samples, and we performed the dynamic analysis on them thanks to cuckoo sandbox which is an automatic analysis tool, from the report generated by the analysis, we extracted the evasion API which represents the suspicious ransomware behavior that determines whether the attack is launched or not, then we used recursive feature elimination (RFE) to keep the most relevant APIs to then test with four machine learning models: decision tree , random forest, k nearest neighbors and support vector machine. The results showed that our approach is highly effective in detecting ransomwares with an accuracy of 98.41%.

Thesis: Diagnosis of covid-19 from medical images using deep learning.

 Coronavirus (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a novel SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus, which affected at the time of the seizure of these lines, 48 million individuals worldwide and caused 1.2 million deaths. Most people infected with the virus suffer from a respiratory disorder, and eventually recover without requiring special treatment. Seniors and those with medical conditions such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes and cancer are more likely to develop serious complications. Although the PCR: polymerase chain reaction test is the most commonly used molecular test. It is very specific, but results are available in a few days to confirm COVID-19 infection. In addition, they are expensive and unavailable in less fortunate countries. RTD Rapid tests detect viral proteins, they are cheaper than PCR and deliver results in minutes, although they are usually less accurate. Due to the importance and demonstrated urgency of such a virus, rapid, affordable and highly accurate testing is required. Therefore, the automatic diagnosis by analysis and recognition of medical images represents an important alternative, which adapts well to the urgency and offers very interesting accuracy rates. It is within the framework of contribution that our contribution is made, to develop an automatic diagnostic system through the use of the latest innovations in deep learning and convolutive neural networks CNN. They have revolutionized the health sector because of their high performance. Deep learning optimizes diagnosis through medical imaging and reduces subjectivity and errors due to distraction, case complexity, misapplication of rules, or lack of knowledge. Chest X-ray imaging is suitable for scanning, accessibility and cost. The purpose of this work is to implement a deep learning approach to diagnose Covid-19 through image analysis viruses are present. The proposed transfer learning system is based on the VGG16 model and achieves a promising accuracy of 98.65%.
